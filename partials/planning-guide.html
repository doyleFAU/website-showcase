How to plan a small business website (step by step)

A practical guide from Vervio — free website planning for business owners.

You do not need to code or hire anyone on day one. This guide shows how to go from a blank idea to a clear homepage plan you can hand to a designer, agency, or DIY website builder.

1. Start with your business type

A restaurant homepage should feel different from a law firm or a gym. Use Quick Start to load a starting point that matches what you do.

2. Pick a homepage layout

Vervio includes 16 homepage ideas — tech, local business, creative studio, fine dining, fitness, travel, photography, medical, nonprofit, and more. Tap through them on mobile or desktop until one feels right.

3. Choose colors and fonts

Open Pick a Style and try themes live. The whole preview updates so you can see how your brand colors and typography work together before you commit.

4. List must-have features

Contact forms, photo galleries, online booking, menus, reviews, maps — check what you need on the Add-ons page and save picks to My List.

5. Share your plan

Your saved list becomes a simple brief. Send it to a web developer, marketing friend, or use it yourself when setting up Wix, Squarespace, WordPress, or a custom build.
